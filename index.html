Level 2 Activity Banks

For A2 Level | 15–20 minutes | Click a card to read

Vocabulary Games

Learn new words and practice memory skills.

1

Flyswatter

Goal: Identify words quickly.

Students race to swat the correct vocabulary words on the board.

Read more
2 clean flyswatters, whiteboard markers.
  1. Write about 15 vocabulary words randomly on the whiteboard.
  2. Divide the class into two teams.
  3. Ask one student from each team to stand at the front. Give them a flyswatter.
  4. Call out a word or describe it clearly.
  5. The first student to swat the correct word wins a point for their team.
If your students are confident, do not just say the word. Instead, give them a clue or a definition so they have to think more.
2

Draw or Act

Goal: Express words creatively.

One student draws or acts out a word while their team tries to guess it.

Read more
Word cards, whiteboard markers.
  1. Divide the class into teams.
  2. One student picks a word card and goes to the front.
  3. They have to draw the word on the board or act it out, but they cannot speak.
  4. Their team has one minute to guess the word correctly.
  5. If they guess it, they earn a point.
Remind students that they cannot write letters or numbers on the board when drawing. They can only draw pictures.
3

Bingo

Goal: Practice listening and spelling.

Students listen to the words you call out and mark them on their grids.

Read more
Paper, pens, a list of vocabulary words.
  1. Write 15 to 20 words on the board.
  2. Ask students to draw a 3x3 grid on their paper.
  3. They can choose any 9 words from the board and write them in their grid.
  4. Call out the words randomly. Students cross them out.
  5. The first student to cross out three words in a line shouts "Bingo!".
Always ask the winner to read their three words out loud to check their answers.
4

Hot Potato

Goal: Recall words under pressure.

Students pass a ball around the circle and say a word before the timer goes off.

Read more
A soft ball, a timer (on a phone).
  1. Ask all students to stand in a big circle.
  2. Set a timer on your phone for about 30 seconds, but hide it.
  3. Give a soft ball to one student.
  4. They must say a vocabulary word and quickly pass the ball to the next person.
  5. The person holding the ball when the timer rings loses the round.
Keep the timer hidden from the students. The game is much more exciting because they do not know when the time will stop.
5

Snowball Fight

Goal: Ice-breaker and reading.

Students write words on paper, crumple them up, and throw them like snowballs.

Read more
Scrap paper, pens.
  1. Give each student a piece of scrap paper.
  2. Ask them to write three vocabulary words on it.
  3. Tell them to crumple the paper tightly into a ball.
  4. Say "Start!" and let them throw the snowballs at each other for 30 seconds.
  5. Say "Stop!" and have everyone pick up one snowball to read the words aloud.
Make a strict rule that they cannot aim at anyone's face. This game is perfect for waking up sleepy students.
6

Guess the Word

Goal: Describe vocabulary.

Students describe a hidden word using simple clues without saying the actual word.

Read more
Word cards.
  1. Ask two students to come to the front.
  2. Student A looks at the board, and Student B looks at the class.
  3. Write a word on the board.
  4. Student A gives simple clues (like colors or opposites) without saying the target word.
  5. Student B tries to guess it based on the clues.
Encourage students to use full sentences for their clues, like "It is a red fruit" instead of just saying "red".
7

Match Game

Goal: Memory and association.

Students take turns flipping cards to find matching pairs of pictures and words.

Read more
Picture cards, word cards, magnets.
  1. Stick picture cards and word cards face down on the board.
  2. Divide the class into teams.
  3. A team chooses two cards to turn over.
  4. If the picture and the word match, they read the word and keep the cards.
  5. If they do not match, turn them face down again.
Write numbers on the backs of the cards so students can easily tell you which cards they want to turn over.
8

Word Relay

Goal: Search and spell quickly.

Teams race to find and circle hidden words on the whiteboard.

Read more
Whiteboard, 2 markers.
  1. Write a large grid of random letters on the board and hide vocabulary words inside it.
  2. Divide the students into two lines.
  3. Say "Go!" and the first student in each line runs to the board to circle one hidden word.
  4. They run back and pass the marker to the next student.
Write a list of the hidden words next to the grid. Students can cross them out after they find them.

Grammar Games

Practice word order and sentence structure.

1

Make a Sentence

Goal: Reorder mixed words.

Groups work together to arrange mixed-up words into correct sentences.

Read more
Word cards, envelopes.
  1. Cut a sentence into separate words and put them in an envelope.
  2. Divide the class into small groups and give each group an envelope.
  3. When you say "Go!", they open it and arrange the words in the correct order.
  4. The first group to read the correct sentence aloud wins.
Use different colored paper for different sentences. This makes it easy to find lost pieces if they fall on the floor.
2

Find Someone

Goal: Ask specific questions.

Students walk around and ask their classmates specific questions to complete a worksheet.

Read more
Worksheet, pens.
  1. Give each student a worksheet with statements like "Likes to eat pizza".
  2. Students have to walk around the classroom and ask their classmates questions.
  3. They ask, "Do you like to eat pizza?".
  4. If someone says "Yes", they write that person's name on their paper.
Tell students they must speak English. They are not allowed to just pass the paper and ask friends to sign it quietly.
3

Tic-Tac-Toe

Goal: Accuracy in grammar.

Teams must make a grammatically correct sentence to place an X or O on the board.

Read more
Whiteboard, markers.
  1. Draw a 3x3 grid on the board and write a target word in each box.
  2. Divide the class into Team X and Team O.
  3. A team chooses a box and must make a grammatically correct sentence using that word.
  4. If the sentence is perfect, they get to place their X or O.
  5. The first team to get 3 in a row wins.
You must be strict as the teacher. If they forget an 's' or an article like 'a', they do not get the box.
4

Board Race

Goal: Write quickly and correctly.

Relay race where students run to the board to write complete and correct sentences.

Read more
Whiteboard, 2 markers.
  1. Divide the whiteboard into two halves and make two teams.
  2. Tell them a grammar rule, like "Write a negative sentence".
  3. One student from each team runs to the board and writes a complete sentence.
  4. The first team with a 100% correct sentence wins a point.
Remind students to use capital letters at the beginning of the sentence and a period at the end.
5

Question Ball

Goal: Answer in full sentences.

Students toss a ball to each other and practice asking and answering questions.

Read more
A soft ball.
  1. Ask students to stand in a circle.
  2. Throw a soft ball to a student and ask them a target question, like "What did you do yesterday?".
  3. The student must answer with a full sentence.
  4. Then, they throw the ball to another friend and ask them a new question.
Write the question structure and the answer pattern on the board so students can look at it if they forget.
6

Buy the Sentence

Goal: Identify grammar mistakes.

Teams use play money to 'buy' sentences they believe have correct grammar.

Read more
Fake money, whiteboard.
  1. Write 10 sentences on the board. Make sure some are correct and some have mistakes.
  2. Give each team some fake money.
  3. Read the sentences one by one and let the teams bid on the sentences they think are correct.
  4. The team that buys the most correct sentences wins.
Give the teams two minutes to discuss the sentences together before the auction begins.
7

Long Sentence

Goal: Expand sentence length.

Students take turns adding one word to build a very long and funny sentence.

Read more
None.
  1. Start the game by saying a short phrase, like "I bought...".
  2. The first student adds one word: "I bought a...".
  3. The next student adds another word: "I bought a shirt...".
  4. Continue around the room to see how long the sentence can get before someone makes a mistake.
Write the words on the whiteboard as the students say them. This visual help makes the game much easier.
8

Stand in Line

Goal: Physical sentence building.

Students hold word cards and physically stand in order to form a correct sentence.

Read more
Big pieces of paper, markers.
  1. Write one word of a sentence on a large piece of paper.
  2. Give one paper to each student in a group.
  3. Tell them they need to stand in a line to form a correct sentence.
  4. When they believe they are in the correct order, they read the sentence aloud together.
To make it more challenging, give one student a paper with a period or a question mark on it.

Conversation Games

Practice speaking with confidence and fluency.

1

Speed Talking

Goal: Improve speaking fluency.

Students talk with a partner for one minute, then rotate to talk with someone new.

Read more
A timer.
  1. Divide the class into two circles: an inside circle and an outside circle.
  2. Students face each other. Give them a topic and let them talk for one minute.
  3. When you say "Change!", the outside circle moves one step to the right.
  4. They start a new conversation with their new partner.
Playing soft background music can help students feel more relaxed and less shy when speaking.
2

Missing Info

Goal: Exchange information.

Partners ask each other questions to find the missing information on their worksheets.

Read more
Partner A and Partner B worksheets.
  1. Put students in pairs (Student A and Student B).
  2. Give them similar worksheets, but each sheet has different information missing.
  3. They must ask each other questions to find the missing answers.
  4. They cannot look at their partner's paper.
Place a tall book or a folder standing up between the students so they cannot cheat and look at the other paper.
3

Roleplay Cards

Goal: Simulate real life.

Students practice real-life situations like talking to a doctor or shopping for clothes.

Read more
Scenario cards.
  1. Give each pair of students a scenario card, such as "Ordering food in a restaurant".
  2. Give them a few minutes to practice a conversation together.
  3. Ask them to come to the front of the classroom.
  4. They must act out their conversation for the class.
Bring simple props to class! Using a hat, a jacket, or a toy phone makes the roleplay much more fun.
4

Find the Lie

Goal: Speak and verify facts.

Students share three facts about themselves, but one is a lie. The class guesses the lie.

Read more
Paper, pens.
  1. Ask students to write down three sentences about themselves.
  2. Two sentences must be true, and one must be a lie.
  3. A student reads their three sentences aloud to the class.
  4. The rest of the class can ask a few questions to guess which statement is the lie.
Tell students to make their lies sound realistic. If they write "I live on the moon", the game will be too easy.
5

Find Your Match

Goal: Interactive speaking.

Students mingle and talk to find the classmate who has the matching half of their card.

Read more
Dialogue cards cut in half.
  1. Cut dialogue cards into two pieces (Question and Answer). Give one piece to each student.
  2. Students have to walk around the room and speak their lines to their classmates.
  3. When they find the person with the matching response, they sit down together.
Make a strict rule that they cannot show their cards to anyone. They must rely entirely on their speaking and listening skills.
6

Pull a Block

Goal: Spontaneous answers.

Students play Jenga. When they pull a block, they answer the matching question.

Read more
Jenga game with numbers, a list of numbered questions.
  1. Write numbers from 1 to 30 on the blocks of a Jenga game.
  2. Give the students a list of numbered questions.
  3. When a student carefully pulls out a block, they must look at the number.
  4. They find the matching question on the list and answer it aloud.
Mix up the questions! Include some easy grammar questions and some funny personal questions to keep it interesting.
7

Be a Star

Goal: Ask continuous questions.

One student pretends to be famous, and the other students act as reporters asking questions.

Read more
Role cards (Singer, Actor, Athlete).
  1. Choose one student to pretend to be a famous person. They sit at the front of the classroom.
  2. The other students act as reporters at a press conference.
  3. The reporters have two minutes to ask the star as many questions as possible.
  4. The star answers creatively, then you switch roles.
Teach the reporters how to say "Follow-up question!" to make the game feel more like a real interview.
8

The "Why" Game

Goal: Explain reasons clearly.

Students try to keep a conversation going by constantly asking their partner "Why?".

Read more
None.
  1. Put students in pairs.
  2. Student A makes a simple statement, like "I like the weekend".
  3. Student B simply asks, "Why?". Student A must provide a reason.
  4. Student B asks "Why?" again, and Student A must give another reason.
  5. They continue until Student A cannot answer.
This game usually gets very silly. Encourage them to laugh and enjoy the activity while they practice giving reasons.

Reading Games

Improve comprehension and reading speed.

1

Jigsaw Reading

Goal: Share information.

Each student reads a different part of a story and explains it to their group.

Read more
A story cut into 3 or 4 parts.
  1. Cut a short story into three or four paragraphs.
  2. Divide the class into small groups and give each student a different paragraph.
  3. They read their part silently.
  4. Then, they take turns explaining their part to the group so everyone understands the whole story.
Make sure the paragraphs have a clear chronological order, like morning, afternoon, and evening, so it is easy to assemble.
2

Run and Read

Goal: Remember sentences.

One student runs to read a text, remembers it, and dictates it to their partner.

Read more
Short text taped to the wall, paper, pens.
  1. Tape a short text to the wall outside the classroom.
  2. Put students in pairs: one Runner and one Writer.
  3. The Runner goes to the text, memorizes a sentence, runs back, and dictates it to the Writer.
  4. The first team to write the text perfectly wins.
Remind the Writers that they should ask questions like "How do you spell that?" if they are not sure.
3

Read and Draw

Goal: Understand details.

Students read a descriptive text and draw a picture based on what they read.

Read more
Descriptive sentences on paper, colored pencils.
  1. Give students a descriptive paragraph, such as "There is a blue house with two trees next to it".
  2. Students read the text quietly by themselves.
  3. They must draw a picture that includes every detail mentioned in the text.
  4. Compare the drawings at the end.
Use prepositions of place (under, next to, behind) and specific colors to test their reading comprehension carefully.
4

Sentence Match

Goal: Connect ideas logically.

Students read and connect the beginning of a sentence to its logical ending.

Read more
Worksheets with split sentences.
  1. Create a worksheet where the beginnings of sentences are on the left side, and the endings are mixed up on the right side.
  2. Students must read both sides.
  3. They draw a line to connect the parts that make logical sense together.
To review the answers, ask one student to read the first half loudly, and another student to read the second half.
5

True or False

Goal: Read and decide fast.

Students read a short text quickly and race to sit on the "True" or "False" chair.

Read more
2 chairs, whiteboard/projector.
  1. Place two chairs at the front of the room and label them "True" and "False".
  2. Project a short story on the whiteboard.
  3. Ask a question about the text.
  4. Two students must quickly read the text, decide the answer, and run to sit on the correct chair.
Use synonyms in your questions. If the text says "happy", ask if the character is "glad" to make them think harder.
6

Story Order

Goal: Understand story sequences.

Groups work together to put the mixed-up sentences of a story in the correct order.

Read more
A story cut into separate sentence strips.
  1. Cut a short story into individual sentence strips.
  2. Give a mixed-up set of strips to small groups of students.
  3. They need to read all the sentences.
  4. They work together to arrange them in the correct logical order from top to bottom.
Choose stories that use clear transition words like "First", "Then", "Next", and "Finally" to help students understand the sequence.
7

Find It Fast

Goal: Scan text efficiently.

Students scan a reading passage quickly to find specific target words or numbers.

Read more
Printed reading passages.
  1. Give every student a copy of the same reading passage.
  2. Call out a specific word or number loudly.
  3. Students must scan the text as fast as they can without reading every word.
  4. The first student to point at the word and read the sentence out loud wins a point.
Start the game by asking them to find capital letters or years, like "London" or "2022", because they stand out visually.
8

Missing Word

Goal: Use context clues.

Students use context clues to choose the correct word from a list to fill in the blanks.

Read more
Worksheet with blank spaces, word bank.
  1. Give students a short story with several blank spaces. Provide a list of words at the top.
  2. Students must read the sentences carefully.
  3. They use context clues to choose the correct word from the list to fill in each blank space.
Complete the first blank space together as a class. Explain how looking at the words before and after the blank can help them guess.

All Skills Review

Combine reading, writing, listening, and speaking.

1

Point Game

Goal: Review everything.

A fun quiz game where teams answer different types of English questions to win points.

Read more
Whiteboard with categories.
  1. Draw a large grid on the board with categories like Vocabulary, Grammar, and Reading.
  2. Assign point values from 100 to 300.
  3. Teams take turns choosing a category. Ask them a question.
  4. If they answer correctly, they win the points for their team.
Make the 300-point questions genuinely challenging. Keep the score updated clearly on the board to keep the competition exciting.
2

Table Rotation

Goal: Practice independently.

Groups rotate around the classroom to complete different reading, writing, and speaking tasks.

Read more
4 tables prepared with different activities.
  1. Set up four tables in the classroom with a different task at each table (Reading, Writing, Listening, Speaking).
  2. Divide the students into four groups.
  3. Give them five minutes to work at their table.
  4. When you ring a bell, they must rotate to the next table.
Make sure the instructions on each table are very clear so you do not have to explain the rules every time they switch.
3

Board Game

Goal: Learn while playing.

Students roll dice and complete English challenges to move their pieces across the board.

Read more
Printed board games, dice, game pieces.
  1. Print a simple board game and give one to each small group of students. Provide dice.
  2. Students roll the dice and move their pieces.
  3. They must successfully complete the English task written on the square they land on to stay there.
Encourage students to help each other. Peer learning is a great way for them to practice outside of direct teacher instruction.
4

Plan a Party

Goal: Collaborate using English.

Groups collaborate to plan a party by writing lists, making invitations, and discussing ideas.

Read more
Paper, colored pens.
  1. Tell the groups they need to plan a fun class party.
  2. They have to work together to write a shopping list for food and discuss which games to play.
  3. They also need to design a party invitation.
  4. Finally, they present their party plan to the rest of the class.
Write a clear checklist on the whiteboard so students know exactly what they need to finish before the time is up.
5

Escape Room

Goal: Solve puzzles together.

Teams solve English puzzles hidden in envelopes to 'escape' the classroom and win.

Read more
Envelopes containing different worksheets.
  1. Put an English puzzle in an envelope.
  2. When a team solves the first puzzle, they give you the answer to get the second envelope.
  3. They must solve four envelopes in total.
  4. The first team to finish all the puzzles 'escapes' the room and wins the game.
If a group is completely stuck on a puzzle, let them sing a short English song to earn a helpful hint from the teacher.
6

The Shop

Goal: Practice practical conversations.

Students practice buying and selling items using play money and simple negotiation phrases.

Read more
Fake money, picture cards.
  1. Half of the class act as shopkeepers selling items on picture cards.
  2. The other half are buyers with fake money and a shopping list.
  3. Buyers walk around the room, ask for prices, and buy items.
  4. After ten minutes, have the students swap roles.
Teach them useful phrases for bargaining before you start, such as "That is too expensive!" or "Can I have a discount?".
7

Pass the Story

Goal: Write creatively.

Students write one sentence, pass their paper, and build a funny story together.

Read more
Paper, pens.
  1. Ask every student to write the beginning sentence of a story at the top of a piece of paper.
  2. Say "Pass!" and students hand their paper to the person next to them.
  3. They read the sentence and add one more.
  4. Repeat this five times, then read the stories aloud.
Give them a funny prompt to start the activity, for example, "A giant alien walked into the supermarket yesterday".
8

Relay Race

Goal: Complete tasks quickly.

Teams race to the front of the class to answer questions on a comprehensive worksheet.

Read more
Worksheets.
  1. Place a comprehensive worksheet on a desk at the front of the classroom.
  2. Put students into teams in lines at the back.
  3. The first student runs to the front, answers only one question on the paper, and runs back to tag their teammate.
  4. The first team to finish the paper correctly wins.
Tape the worksheet securely to the desk so it does not fly away when students are rushing back and forth.

Listening Games

Train students to catch important information.

1

Listen & Draw

Goal: Understand descriptions.

Students listen to the teacher's description and try to draw exactly what they hear.

Read more
Paper, pens.
  1. Describe a strange or funny picture slowly and clearly, like "Draw a monster with three arms and a big hat".
  2. Students must listen carefully and draw what they hear without looking at their classmates' papers.
  3. Show them your original picture at the end to compare.
Use familiar vocabulary like colors, body parts, and prepositions to make sure they can focus on listening rather than guessing words.
2

Up and Down

Goal: Listen for specific categories.

Students listen to a list of words and stand up or sit down when they hear specific categories.

Read more
A prepared list of words.
  1. Assign a physical rule to the class, for example, "Stand up when you hear an animal, and sit down when you hear a color".
  2. Read a list of words or a short story aloud.
  3. Students must physically react as soon as they hear the target words.
Start reading slowly, and then gradually increase your speed. This makes the game much more challenging and fun.
3

Grab the Card

Goal: Process audio quickly.

Students listen carefully and race to slap the correct picture card on the table.

Read more
Picture cards spread on a table.
  1. Place several vocabulary picture cards face up on a table.
  2. Put students in small groups around the table.
  3. Call out a word clearly.
  4. The first student to slap their hand on the correct picture card gets to keep it. The student with the most cards wins.
Tell students to keep their hands on their heads before you say the word to prevent them from hovering their hands over the cards.
4

The Telephone

Goal: Practice pronunciation and listening.

Students whisper a sentence down a line to see if the final message matches the original.

Read more
Target sentences.
  1. Have students sit in a straight line or a circle.
  2. Whisper a sentence to the first student.
  3. That student whispers it to the next person. They can only say it once!
  4. The last student in line says the sentence out loud to see if it matches the original.
Use sentences with words that sound similar or funny tongue twisters to make the listening task more difficult and entertaining.
5

Listen & Color

Goal: Follow detailed instructions.

Students listen to instructions and color specific parts of a picture accordingly.

Read more
Printed pictures, colored pencils.
  1. Give every student the same black-and-white coloring page.
  2. Give them verbal instructions, like "Color the boy's shoes red".
  3. Students must listen to your instructions and color the specific parts of the picture correctly.
  4. Check their pictures afterwards.
Wait about ten seconds between each instruction to give the students enough time to find the right colored pencil and color the area.
6

Simon Says

Goal: Listen for a key phrase.

Students must listen carefully and only follow the instructions if they hear "Simon says".

Read more
None.
  1. Give the class physical commands. If you say "Simon says touch your nose", they must do it.
  2. If you only say "Touch your nose" without saying "Simon says", they must stand still.
  3. Anyone who does the action at the wrong time is out of the game.
To trick the students, perform the wrong action while giving a command, like touching your ear while saying "touch your nose".
7

Song Blanks

Goal: Understand spoken English in songs.

Students listen to a popular English song and try to fill in the missing words on their paper.

Read more
Song lyrics with blanks, music player.
  1. Print out the lyrics to a popular English song, but leave several blank spaces.
  2. Play the song for the class.
  3. Students must listen carefully and try to write the missing words in the blanks.
  4. Review the answers together and sing the song.
Choose acoustic or slower pop songs. Fast rap or rock songs are usually too difficult for A2 students to understand clearly.
8

Follow the Map

Goal: Follow spatial directions.

Students listen to directions and trace the route on a map to find the secret destination.

Read more
Printed maps for each student.
  1. Give each student a printed street map. Tell them to put their finger on the starting point.
  2. Give them directions aloud, such as "Go straight and turn left at the park".
  3. Students follow the route on the map and shout out their final destination when you stop.
Draw a large version of the map on the whiteboard. After the game, show them the correct path so they can see if they made a mistake.
